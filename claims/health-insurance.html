Types of Claims

Cashless vs Reimbursement Claims

Most health insurance claims fall into one of these two categories. Knowing which one applies to you is the first step.

Cashless Claim

Available at network hospitals. The insurer settles the bill directly with the hospital — you only pay for non-medical items (toiletries, attendant charges, etc). Requires pre-authorisation.

Reimbursement Claim

You pay the hospital bill upfront — at any hospital, network or non-network — then submit bills, reports and discharge summary to the insurer for repayment.

Step by Step

Cashless Claim Process (Network Hospital)

For planned admissions, start this process at least 2-3 days in advance. For emergencies, the hospital's insurance desk can usually start it on arrival.

1

Choose a network hospital

Check your insurer's network hospital list (or ask Policy Aid) and confirm the hospital accepts cashless treatment for your insurer.

2

Show your health card / policy details

At the hospital's insurance/TPA desk, show your e-card or policy number along with a photo ID.

3

Hospital sends a pre-authorisation request

The hospital fills out a cashless request form with diagnosis and estimated cost, and sends it to the insurer/TPA.

4

Insurer approves (or queries) the request

Approval typically takes 1-4 hours for planned admissions and up to 6 hours for emergencies. The insurer may ask for more medical details before approving.

5

Get treated — pay only non-medical extras

The insurer settles the approved bill directly with the hospital at discharge. You pay only items not covered (e.g. admin charges, food for attendants).

Step by Step

Reimbursement Claim Process

Use this process if you were treated at a non-network hospital, or if a cashless request was not approved in time.

1

Inform the insurer within 24-48 hours

Most policies require claim intimation within 24-48 hours of admission (immediately for emergencies, before admission for planned treatment).

2

Pay the hospital bill and collect documents

Pay all charges and collect the discharge summary, original bills, payment receipts, and diagnostic reports before leaving the hospital.

3

Fill the claim form

Complete the insurer's reimbursement claim form (available online or via Policy Aid) with treatment and bank details for the payout.

4

Submit the claim

Submit the form with all documents to the insurer/TPA online, by email, or at a branch — within the time limit specified in your policy (usually 15-30 days from discharge).

5

Track and follow up

The insurer may raise queries for additional documents. Once approved, the amount is credited to your registered bank account — typically within 7-30 days.

Documents

Documents You'll Need

Keep these ready to avoid delays — for both cashless and reimbursement claims.

At Admission

  • Health insurance card / policy copy
  • Government photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, etc.)
  • Doctor's admission advice / referral letter

At Discharge

  • Discharge summary
  • Original hospital bills & payment receipts
  • Diagnostic reports (lab, imaging, etc.)
  • Pharmacy bills with prescriptions

For Reimbursement

  • Duly filled claim form
  • Cancelled cheque / bank details
  • KYC documents (if requested)
  • FIR copy (for accident-related claims)

Tip: Always keep photocopies or scanned copies of every document before submitting originals to the hospital or insurer.

Avoid Rejections

Common Reasons Claims Get Rejected

Most rejections are avoidable. Here's what to watch out for.

1

Non-disclosure at purchase

Not declaring pre-existing conditions or lifestyle habits (smoking, etc.) when buying the policy can lead to claim denial.

2

Claim during waiting period

Specific illnesses, pre-existing conditions and maternity have waiting periods (1-4 years) — claims within this period are rejected.

3

Late intimation

Not informing the insurer within the required time window (usually 24-48 hours) can result in rejection or delays.

4

Excluded treatments

Cosmetic procedures, certain OPD treatments and some alternative therapies may be excluded — check your policy wording.

FAQ

Common Questions About Claims

Straight answers — no jargon.

How long does a health insurance claim take to settle?
Cashless claims are typically pre-authorised within 1-4 hours for planned admissions and within 6 hours for emergencies. Reimbursement claims usually take 7-30 days after all documents are submitted, depending on the insurer.
What is the difference between cashless and reimbursement claims?
In a cashless claim, the insurer settles the bill directly with a network hospital — you pay only non-medical expenses. In a reimbursement claim, you pay the hospital first and then submit bills and documents to the insurer for repayment.
Why do health insurance claims get rejected?
Common reasons include non-disclosure of pre-existing conditions, claims during the waiting period, treatment at a non-network hospital without prior intimation, incomplete documentation, and treatments excluded under the policy.
Can Policy Aid help me file or follow up on a claim?
