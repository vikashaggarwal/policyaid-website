Student Travel Insurance: What Indian Students Studying Abroad Need to Know

January 2026 · 9 min read · By Policy Aid

Every year, over 7.5 lakh Indian students go abroad for higher education. Most universities require health insurance — but university health plans are often expensive and limited. Here's a complete guide to student travel insurance from India.

💰 Students who buy insurance from India save 40–70% compared to university health plans — and pay in INR with no forex charges.

Why Buy from India Instead of Abroad?

  • Significantly cheaper — save 40–70% vs university health plans
  • Pay in INR — no forex conversion charges
  • Covers the journey to and from India as well
  • Familiar claims process with Hindi/English support
  • Easier for parents to manage renewals from India

Must-Have Covers for Students

  • Medical and hospitalisation cover: Minimum $1,00,000
  • Personal liability cover (required by some universities)
  • Loss of study fees due to medical emergency
  • Sponsor protection cover (if your sponsor passes away)
  • Compassionate visit — covers a family member flying to you in emergency

Destination-Wise Recommendations

  • USA / Canada – Get at least $5,00,000 medical cover. Healthcare is extremely expensive.
  • UK – NHS covers emergencies but not all treatments. Get a plan with OPD cover.
  • Australia – OSHC (Overseas Student Health Cover) is mandatory. We help you buy the right plan.
  • Europe (Schengen) – Minimum €30,000 medical cover required for visa.
