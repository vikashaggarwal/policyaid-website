What Is Not Covered in Health Insurance: 15 Common Exclusions

March 2026 · 7 min read · By Policy Aid

Most people only read their health insurance policy when they need to make a claim — and that's when they discover the exclusions. Here are 15 common things NOT covered by most health insurance plans in India.

  1. Pre-existing diseases – Usually excluded for 2–4 years from policy start date
  2. Cosmetic surgery – Unless required due to accident or illness
  3. Dental treatment – Unless caused by accident (OPD dental usually excluded)
  4. Spectacles and hearing aids – Unless covered by add-on rider
  5. Pregnancy and childbirth – Most plans have a 9-month to 2-year waiting period
  6. Self-inflicted injuries – Including injuries from substance abuse
  7. War and nuclear risks – Standard exclusion across all plans
  8. Experimental treatments – Unproven or investigational therapies
  9. Rest cure and obesity treatment – Weight-loss programs, dietary supplements
  10. External aids and appliances – Crutches, wheelchairs etc. (unless specified)
  11. Vaccination costs – Preventive vaccines are usually not covered
  12. Outpatient consultations – Unless OPD cover is specifically included
  13. Specific diseases in first year – Hernia, cataract, joint replacement etc.
  14. Non-allopathic treatment – Ayurveda, homeopathy (unless AYUSH cover added)
  15. Room rent above limit – Many plans cap room rent at 1–2% of sum insured/day
