Plan Types

Which Travel Insurance Plan Do You Need?

Three main types — choose based on how often you travel and your destination.

Single Trip

Covers one international or domestic trip from departure to return. Choose your destination and duration — policy is issued instantly.

Best for: Occasional travellers, first-time buyers

Multi-Trip Annual

Covers unlimited trips in a year, each up to 30–45 days. Much more economical for frequent flyers — one premium, all trips covered.

Best for: Business travellers, 4+ trips per year

Student / Study Abroad

Designed for students studying overseas. Covers medical treatment, mental health support and personal liability for the full academic year.

Best for: Students in US, UK, Canada, Australia

What's Covered — and What's Usually Excluded

A comprehensive travel policy bundles several distinct types of protection. Here's what to expect.

Emergency Medical Treatment

Hospitalisation, surgery, doctor consultations abroad

Medical Evacuation

Emergency transport to the nearest adequate facility, or repatriation to India

Baggage Loss/Delay

Compensation for lost, stolen or significantly delayed checked baggage

Flight Delay/Cancellation

Fixed payouts for delays beyond a threshold (e.g. 6+ hours)

Passport Loss

Cost of obtaining an emergency replacement passport abroad

Trip Cancellation/Curtailment

Reimbursement for non-refundable costs if a covered reason forces cancellation

Personal Liability

Covers legal liability if you accidentally injure someone or damage property

Hijack/Detention Cover

Daily allowance if your flight is hijacked or you're detained

Common exclusions to watch for

  • Pre-existing medical conditions — usually excluded unless a specific PED add-on is purchased.
  • Travel against medical advice — if you travel despite a doctor advising against it, related claims will be denied.
  • Adventure sports — activities like skiing, scuba diving or bungee jumping often need a separate add-on.
  • Travel to countries under government advisory — claims may be void if you travel to a region India has issued an advisory against.
  • Self-inflicted injury, intoxication-related incidents, and war/terrorism (though some policies do cover terrorism — check specifically).
  • Cosmetic, dental (beyond emergency), and routine check-ups — travel insurance is for emergencies, not routine care.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers on travel insurance in India.

Is travel insurance mandatory for international travel?
Travel insurance is mandatory for Schengen visa countries (Europe). For other destinations it is not legally required but strongly recommended — a single overseas hospitalisation can cost ₹5–20 lakh or more without cover.
How much does international travel insurance cost?
A standard 15-day international plan starts from ₹500–800 per person. Annual multi-trip plans for frequent travellers cost ₹3,000–6,000 per year. Premium depends on your age, destination and cover amount.
Does travel insurance cover pre-existing medical conditions?
Most standard plans exclude treatment of pre-existing conditions abroad. However, emergency stabilisation is usually covered. Some premium plans offer limited pre-existing disease cover — our advisors can guide you to the right option.
Can I buy travel insurance after I start my trip?
No — travel insurance must be purchased before your departure. The policy cannot be backdated. Contact Policy Aid if you're departing soon and need a policy issued immediately.
Is domestic travel insurance worth buying?
Yes, especially for air travel. Domestic travel insurance covers trip cancellation, flight delay and baggage loss at a very low cost (₹200–400 per trip). For train or bus travel, evaluate based on the trip value and your risk appetite.
