Plan Types

Types of Travel Insurance

Choose a plan that matches your travel frequency, destination, and purpose of travel.

Single Trip

Ideal for occasional travellers making one international or domestic trip. Coverage begins on departure and ends on return.

  • Per trip pricing — pay only for the days you travel
  • Flexible trip duration options (7, 15, 30, 60, 90 days)
  • Individual, couple, or family floater options
  • Available for international and domestic travel
POPULAR

Multi-Trip Annual

One policy, unlimited trips throughout the year. Cost-effective for travellers who make 3 or more trips in a year.

  • Unlimited trips within 12 months
  • Maximum trip duration per journey (30-90 days)
  • Significant savings vs buying per-trip policies
  • Covers both international and domestic trips

Student / Education Abroad

Designed for Indian students studying at universities and colleges outside India. Longer duration cover with student-specific benefits.

  • Coverage up to 1-2 years duration
  • Medical and hospitalisation cover abroad
  • Study interruption cover
  • Sponsor protection in case of parent's death

What's Covered

Travel Insurance Covers at a Glance

A comprehensive travel plan protects you against the most common and costly travel disruptions.

Medical Emergency Abroad

Covers hospitalisation, surgery, doctor consultations, and emergency medical evacuation. Up to $5,00,000 depending on plan. Cashless facility at network hospitals overseas.

Trip Cancellation

Reimburses non-refundable pre-paid trip costs (flights, hotels, tour bookings) if you cancel your trip due to serious illness, death in family, natural disaster, or other covered reasons.

Lost Baggage

Compensates for permanent loss of checked-in baggage by the airline. Also covers delayed baggage — reimbursing emergency purchases of essential items while you wait.

Flight Delay

Pays a fixed allowance per hour if your flight is delayed beyond the threshold (usually 6-12 hours). Covers meals, accommodation, and incidental expenses during the delay.

Passport Loss

Covers expenses incurred for obtaining an emergency travel document or replacement passport while abroad, including administrative fees and local travel costs.

Personal Liability

Covers your legal liability if you accidentally injure a third party or cause damage to their property while abroad. Pays legal costs and compensation up to the sum insured.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions travellers ask most often before buying cover.

Travel insurance is not legally mandatory for all destinations, but it is a visa requirement for Schengen countries (Europe) and several other nations. Even where it is not mandatory, it is strongly recommended as medical treatment abroad can cost lakhs of rupees without cover.
A single-trip policy covers one specific journey from departure to return. A multi-trip annual policy covers unlimited trips within a 12-month period, subject to a maximum duration per trip (usually 30 to 90 days per trip). Frequent travellers save significantly with an annual policy.
Most standard travel insurance plans exclude pre-existing conditions. However, some insurers offer plans that cover stable pre-existing conditions subject to declaration and additional premium. It is essential to disclose all health conditions at the time of purchase to avoid claim rejection.
You can buy travel insurance right up to the day before departure, but buying early is advisable. Purchasing well in advance means you are covered for trip cancellation arising from events between purchase and departure, such as illness, bereavement, or unforeseen circumstances.
