Coverage

What does Health Insurance cover?

A standard health insurance plan in India typically covers the following expenses.

Hospitalisation expenses

Room rent, nursing charges, surgeon fees, anaesthetist fees, and all in-patient treatment costs for illness or injury requiring more than 24 hours of admission.

Pre & post hospitalisation

Medical costs incurred before admission (typically 30–60 days) and after discharge (typically 60–90 days), including diagnostics, consultations, and medicines.

Day-care procedures

Over 400 procedures such as cataract surgery, chemotherapy, and dialysis that require less than 24 hours at a hospital are covered under day-care provisions.

No-claim bonus

Each claim-free year rewards you with an automatic increase in your sum insured — typically 10–50% — at no additional premium, building stronger protection over time.

Optional add-on

Maternity cover

Covers delivery expenses (normal and caesarean), pre-natal and post-natal costs, and newborn cover. A waiting period of 2–4 years usually applies.

Optional add-on

Critical illness cover

Pays a lump sum on diagnosis of specified critical illnesses such as cancer, heart attack, or stroke. Acts as a supplement to your base hospitalisation plan.

Plan types

Types of Health Insurance

Choose the plan type that fits your household and budget.

Individual Plan

A dedicated sum insured for one person. Ideal for working professionals or anyone seeking independent cover. The entire sum insured is available solely for that individual.

Best for: Self

Family Floater Plan

One shared sum insured covers the entire family — you, your spouse, and children — under a single policy. More economical than buying separate individual policies for each member.

Best for: Families

Senior Citizen Plan

Designed for parents aged 60 and above. Offers higher coverage for age-related ailments, pre-existing disease cover after a short waiting period, and domiciliary care benefits.

Best for: Parents 60+

FAQ

Common questions before buying

Health insurance is a contract between you and an insurer that covers medical expenses arising from illness or injury. With rising hospitalisation costs in India, a single admission can cost ₹1–5 lakh or more. Health insurance protects your savings and gives you access to quality care without financial stress. It also provides tax benefits under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act.
An individual plan covers one person with a dedicated sum insured. A family floater plan covers the entire family — spouse, children, and sometimes parents — under a single shared sum insured, usually at a lower combined premium. Family floaters are cost-effective when family members rarely fall ill simultaneously. However, if a senior member makes a large claim, the shared pool reduces cover for others.
A no-claim bonus (NCB) is a reward given by insurers when you do not make a claim in a policy year. Your sum insured increases — typically by 10–50% — at no extra cost. Over several claim-free years, your coverage can grow significantly without any additional premium. Some plans also offer a cumulative bonus, which continues to accumulate across multiple years.
A sum insured of ₹5–10 lakh is the recommended minimum for a metro city individual. For families or cities with higher medical costs, ₹15–25 lakh is advisable. Consider your city tier, the hospitals you wish to access, your age, and existing health conditions. You can also top up a base plan with a super top-up to get higher coverage at a lower premium.
