What's Covered

Comprehensive Motor Insurance Covers

A standard comprehensive policy protects you against a wide range of risks beyond just accidents.

Own Damage

Covers repair costs to your vehicle arising from accidents, collisions, and road incidents. Cashless facility available at network garages.

Third-Party Liability

Pays for damage or injury caused to another person, vehicle, or property. Unlimited liability cover for third-party bodily injury or death.

Theft

If your vehicle is stolen and not recovered, the insurer pays the Insured Declared Value (IDV) of the vehicle at the time of policy issuance.

Natural Disasters

Damage caused by floods, cyclones, earthquakes, lightning, storms, or other natural calamities is covered under a comprehensive policy.

Personal Accident

Compulsory personal accident cover of ₹15 lakh for the owner-driver. Covers permanent disability and accidental death caused while driving.

Roadside Assistance

24x7 emergency assistance for flat tyres, battery jump-start, fuel delivery, towing, and minor repairs — available as an optional add-on.

Add-Ons

Add-Ons Worth Buying

Enhance your base policy with these popular riders to get truly complete protection for your vehicle.

Zero Depreciation

Standard policies deduct depreciation on replaced parts during claims. With this add-on, you receive the full cost of parts without any depreciation deduction — maximising your claim amount significantly.

Best for vehicles up to 5 years

Engine Protect

Covers repair or replacement costs of the engine and gearbox caused by water ingression (hydrostatic lock) or lubricant oil leakage — common during monsoon flooding. Not covered under standard comprehensive plans.

Must-have in flood-prone cities

Return to Invoice

In case of total loss or theft, the standard payout is the IDV, which is lower than your original purchase price. This add-on ensures you receive the full on-road price, bridging the gap between IDV and invoice value.

Ideal for new vehicles

No-Claim Bonus Protect

Your earned NCB discount (up to 50%) is preserved even if you make one claim during the policy year. Without this, a single claim resets your NCB to zero, costing you significantly on future premiums.

Protect your 50% NCB

Zero depreciation (also called nil depreciation or bumper-to-bumper) is an add-on that ensures your insurer pays the full cost of replaced parts without deducting depreciation. It is especially valuable for vehicles up to 5 years old.
NCB is a discount on your own-damage premium for every claim-free year. It starts at 20% after the first claim-free year and goes up to 50% after five consecutive claim-free years. NCB belongs to the owner, not the vehicle, so it transfers if you buy a new car.
The NCB discount can be transferred to your new car from the old one. However, the policy itself needs to be issued fresh for the new vehicle. Policy Aid advisors can help you carry forward your NCB and choose the right plan for your new vehicle.
