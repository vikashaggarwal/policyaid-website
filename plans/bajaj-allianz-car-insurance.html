What makes Bajaj Allianz Car Insurance stand out

Most Affordable Comprehensive Plan

At around ₹4,500/year, Bajaj Allianz offers the cheapest comprehensive cover among major insurers for a typical hatchback.

Pay As You Drive (Flexible Plan)

A usage-based plan combining Depreciation Shield and Engine Protect, ideal for low-mileage drivers who want to save on premium.

~98% Claim Settlement Ratio

A strong claim settlement track record means your claims are highly likely to be honoured promptly.

4,000+ Cashless Garages

Repair your car without paying upfront at over 4,000 preferred network garages across India.

75% On-Account Payment Outside Network

Even if you choose a garage outside the network, Bajaj Allianz pays 75% of the estimated claim amount upfront.

Bumper-to-Bumper Cover

Engine Protect Add-on

Covers engine, gearbox and transmission damage caused by water ingression and oil leakage.

Instant Claim Assistance with SMS Updates

Real-time SMS notifications keep you informed at every stage of your claim, from registration to settlement.

Who Should Buy Bajaj Allianz Car Insurance?

This plan is best suited for the following profiles

Budget-Conscious Car Owners

At around ₹4,500/year, this is the most affordable comprehensive plan among major insurers - ideal if premium cost is your top priority.

Low-Mileage & Occasional Drivers

The Pay As You Drive (Flexible Plan) lets infrequent drivers pay based on usage while still getting Depreciation Shield and Engine Protect benefits.

Owners Who Want Standalone OD Cover

If you already have third-party-only cover from your dealer, Bajaj Allianz’s Standalone Own Damage policy is a cost-effective top-up.

Competitive pricing combined with a solid ~98% claim settlement ratio makes this a safe, value-driven choice for new car owners.

Pros and Cons

An honest assessment of Bajaj Allianz Car Insurance

Advantages

  • Most affordable comprehensive premium among major insurers (~₹4,500/year)
  • Strong claim settlement ratio of approximately 98%
  • Flexible Pay As You Drive plan for low-mileage drivers
  • 75% on-account payment even at non-network garages
  • Instant claim assistance with real-time SMS updates
  • Wide range of add-ons including Bumper-to-Bumper and Electrical Accessories cover
  • Standalone Own Damage option available

Disadvantages

  • Smaller cashless network (4,000+) compared to SBI General or Reliance General
  • Pay As You Drive plan may not suit high-mileage or daily commuters
  • Most valuable add-ons cost extra over the base premium
  • Claim ratio slightly behind HDFC ERGO’s industry-leading figure
