How to Choose the Right Car Insurance Plan

1. Comprehensive vs Third-Party vs Standalone OD

Third-party cover is legally mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, and its premium is fixed by IRDAI. Comprehensive cover adds protection for your own vehicle against accidents, theft, fire and natural disasters. If you already have third-party cover and just want own-damage protection, a Standalone Own Damage policy — offered by HDFC ERGO, Bajaj Allianz, ICICI Lombard, Tata AIG and others — can be a cost-effective add.

2. Check the Claim Settlement Ratio

HDFC ERGO and Digit currently report among the highest claim settlement ratios (around 99.4% and 97.2% respectively), while SBI General's incurred claim ratio of 102.07% indicates the insurer pays out more in claims than it collects in premiums for motor — generally a good sign for policyholders. A higher ratio means a smoother claims experience when you actually need it.

3. Garage Network & Cashless Repairs

SBI General (20,000+ garages), Reliance General (8,500+) and Tata AIG (7,500+) lead on network size, which matters most if you travel frequently or live outside major metros. A nearby cashless garage means you don't pay upfront and wait for reimbursement.

4. Add-Ons Worth Paying For

Zero depreciation is the most valuable add-on for cars under 5 years old — it ensures full claim payouts without depreciation deductions. Engine protect is essential in flood-prone cities like Mumbai and Chennai. Return to Invoice (RTI) is useful for newer cars in case of total loss or theft, paying the on-road price rather than the depreciated IDV.

5. Set Your IDV Correctly

Insured Declared Value (IDV) is the maximum amount your insurer will pay in case of total loss or theft. Setting it too low reduces your premium but also your payout — always set IDV close to your car's actual current market value, especially for cars under 3 years old.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the best car insurance plan in India 2026? +
HDFC ERGO and Digit lead on claim settlement ratio (around 99.4% and 97.2% respectively), Bajaj Allianz offers a flexible Pay As You Drive option with a strong garage network, and SBI General has one of India's largest networks with 20,000+ garages. The best plan depends on your car's age, IDV and the add-ons you need — our advisors compare all 10 for free.
Which car insurance has the highest claim settlement ratio? +
HDFC ERGO is among the highest at around 99.4%, followed by Digit at 97.2% and Bajaj Allianz at around 98%. SBI General reports an incurred claim ratio of 102.07%, meaning claim payouts exceed premiums collected for motor — generally favourable for policyholders.
How much does car insurance cost in India? +
For a comprehensive policy on a mid-size hatchback (IDV around ₹4,00,000–5,00,000, 5 years old, Mumbai, 20% NCB), premiums typically range from approximately ₹4,400 (Royal Sundaram) to ₹5,400 (Tata AIG Auto Secure). Third-party-only premiums are fixed by IRDAI and start around ₹2,094 for private cars. Premiums vary by car model, age, city, IDV and add-ons selected.
What is the difference between comprehensive and third-party car insurance? +
Third-party insurance is legally mandatory and only covers damage or injury you cause to others. Comprehensive insurance covers third-party liability plus damage to your own vehicle from accidents, theft, fire and natural disasters, and can be customised with add-ons like zero depreciation, engine protect and roadside assistance.
