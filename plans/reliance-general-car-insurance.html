Plan Highlights

What makes Reliance General Private Car Insurance stand out

8,500+ Network Garages

One of the larger cashless garage networks in India, offering convenient access to repairs across major cities and towns.

Pay As You Drive Policy

A new-generation usage-based insurance variant where premiums are linked to actual kilometres driven - ideal for low-mileage drivers.

Value-for-Money Plans

Popular among first-time car owners for offering solid comprehensive coverage at a competitive premium point.

Zero Depreciation Add-on

Ensures full claim payouts on replaced parts without depreciation deductions, recommended for cars under 5 years old.

Engine Protect Cover

Covers engine and gearbox damage from water ingression and oil leakage - useful during monsoon season in Mumbai.

24x7 Roadside Assistance

Round-the-clock support for breakdowns, towing, flat tyres and emergency fuel delivery as an add-on cover.

Strong Digital Claims Process

Reliance General offers a streamlined digital claims journey with online tracking and status updates.

Tyre Protect Cover

Covers cost of tyre and tube replacement due to accidental damage, cuts or bursts - an often-overlooked but valuable add-on.

Pros and Cons

An honest assessment of Reliance General Private Car Insurance

Advantages

  • Large network of 8,500+ cashless garages
  • New-generation Pay As You Drive policy for usage-based savings
  • Value-for-money pricing popular with first-time car owners
  • Strong digital claims process with online tracking
  • Comprehensive add-on suite including Zero Dep, Engine Protect, Tyre Protect
  • Personal accident cover included as standard
  • Standalone Third-Party and comprehensive variants both available

Disadvantages

  • Claim ratio (~94% estimate) trails the top performers like HDFC ERGO and Digit
  • Premium is mid-range - not the cheapest nor the most feature-rich option
  • Add-ons like Zero Dep and Engine Protect cost extra over the base premium
  • Customer service experience can vary by region
