How to Choose the Right Travel Insurance Plan

1. Match Sum Insured to Your Destination

Schengen countries mandate a minimum of €30,000 (~$33,000) medical cover. The USA and Canada have the highest healthcare costs globally — a single hospitalisation can run into lakhs of dollars, so opt for $250,000–$500,000 cover for these destinations. Care International and Tata AIG offer cover up to $1 million for high-risk trips.

2. Check the Claim Settlement Ratio

New India Assurance leads at 96.4%, followed closely by HDFC Ergo at 95.3% and Bajaj Allianz at 93.54%. A higher ratio means fewer claim rejections during a medical emergency abroad — when you need your insurer to respond fast.

3. Single Trip vs Multi-Trip Annual

If you travel internationally 4 or more times a year, a multi-trip annual plan (like Digit's, starting around ₹2,999/year for $500,000 cover) works out far cheaper than buying single-trip cover each time. Each trip under a multi-trip plan is typically capped at 30–45 days.

4. Look Beyond Medical Cover

Trip cancellation, missed connections, baggage loss and flight delay covers vary significantly between insurers. IndusInd Travel Care includes a free international SIM and even covers home burglary in India while you're away — a unique benefit not offered elsewhere.

5. Entry Age Limits Matter for Senior Travellers

Most insurers cap entry age at 70 years, but IndusInd (Reliance) Travel Care covers travellers up to 90 years — important if you're buying cover for elderly parents visiting children abroad. Care Health has no maximum entry age at all.

What is the difference between single-trip and multi-trip travel insurance? +
A single-trip plan covers one journey from departure to return and is priced based on trip duration. A multi-trip (annual) plan covers unlimited international trips within a year, each capped at 30–45 days — much better value for travellers making 4+ trips a year.
Is travel insurance mandatory for Schengen visa applications? +
Yes. Schengen visa rules require a minimum of €30,000 (~$33,000) medical cover valid across all Schengen countries for the full duration of your trip. All 10 plans compared here meet or exceed this requirement.
